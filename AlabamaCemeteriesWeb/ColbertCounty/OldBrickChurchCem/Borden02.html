BORDON, James "Bud" Garth, 20 Dec 1945 - 5 Aug 2009. U. marker: Bradford's Lawrence Funeral Home Moulton, Alabama". (Lawrence Co., Al. native; s/o W.H. and Cora Suddieth Borden; worked for Pilgim's Pride; h/o Martha Carol Romans Borden.) 7310  obit
 
Funeral for James Garth "Bud" Borden, 63, of Moulton will be Saturday, Aug. 8, 2009, at 2 p.m. at Lawrence Funeral Home with Bro. Jimmy Fuller officiating. 
Burial will be in Old Brick Cemetery. Visitation will be tonight from 6 to 8 at the funeral home.
Mr. Borden died Wednesday, Aug. 5, 2009, at Shoals Hospital. He was born Dec. 20, 1945, in Lawrence County to W.H. Borden and Cora Suddieth Borden. He was a laborer for Pilgim's Pride. 
Survivors include three brothers, Junior Borden of Bessemer, Jackie Borden of Decatur and Ronnie Borden of Moulton; two sisters, Shirley Cross of Courtland and Ruth Hughes of Moulton; two stepdaughters, Margie Hood and Sue Swims; two stepgrandchildren; and several nieces and nephews. 
Published in the Decatur Daily on 8/7/2009 
 
 
BORDEN, Martha, 16 Mar 1942 - 27 Jun 1998. (Nee: Martha Carol Romans; Ford City native; d/o John and Lula Romans; w/o James Borden.) 7311 obit
 
MOULTON -- Funeral for Martha Carol Borden, 56, of Moulton will be Tuesday at 2 p.m. at Old Brick Baptist Church with Keith Jones officiating and Elliot Funeral Home directing.
Burial will be in Old Brick Cemetery. The body will be at the church one hour before the funeral.
Mrs. Borden died Saturday, June 27, 1998, at Medical Center Shoals. She was born March 16, 1942, in Ford City to John and Lula Romans. She was a homemaker.
She is survived by her husband, James Borden; two daughters, Sue Swins of Natural Bridge and Margie Hood of Moulton; four brothers, Richard Romans and McKinley Romans, both of Tennessee, Charlie Romans of Virginia and James Romans of Russellville; four sisters, Blanch McCary of Moulton, Ruby Stephson, of Tennessee, and Modine Genero and Flora Martin, both of Rockport, Ill.; six grandchildren; and two great-grandchildren.
Pallbearers will be Daniel Peoples, Robert Brooks, Ronnie Borden, Jackie Borden, Tracie Culver and Tony Jones.
DECATUR DAILY - June 29, 1998
