MASSENGALE CEMETERY, Hurricane Road, MADISON COUNTY ALABAMA

Mapping the Location


MASSENGALE, Mahala, 21 Sep 1823 - 6 Aug 1846, My peace is made, All is well: I'm going home, With Christ to dwell.  (Mahala Massengale's upright tombstone was broken by the pressure up against it from an old tree before it decayed. Her stone was lying face down when I found it.) 0948C / 0948 / footstone M. M.: 0945C / 0945 / 0949 / 0950
 In Memory of Mahala Massengale, Born Sept. 21, 1823, Died Aug. 6, 1846, My peace is made, All is well: I'm going home, With Christ to dwell.

SISCO, Zech W., 24 Mar 1814 - 28 Jan 1844. (nee Zachariah W. Sisco; his tombstone is a hand carved fieldstone that is no longer found in this cemetery.) 0951F
 ZECH x W x SISCO was Born March th 24 1814, Died Jan. th 28 1844.

At the foot of Mahala's grave is an adult's grave marked only with two unlettered fieldstones. One is the headstone (west) and the other the footstone (east).  0946
Other fieldstones marking graves: 0947 / 0951 / 0952/ 0955

 All the "S's" were reversed on the Sisco and the Massengale stones indicating the same tombstone inscriber did both stones.

There were many unmarked graves, in this nearly quarter acre plot. The Mahala Massengale stone is now divided across two property lines of two neighbors. Maybe the original line was moved at one time to the east into the near center of Mahala's grave after a survey.

After "Zech" Zachariah's death Margaret E. married Matthew Massengale on Jan 26 1853.
Matthew was found to be M. Massengale, age 28, born in Ala., a school teacher, living with the Mary Daniel family during the 1850 census of 20 Nov 1850.
