HIGGINBOTHAM aka McCALEB/FISK CEMETERY, Grimwood Road, MADISON COUNTY, ALABAMA


HIGGINBOTHAM, Robert, 1752 - 1825 Rev War Soldier. Fought in battles that were considered the turning point of the Revolutionary War against the British. Two of those were King's Mountain / Guilford Courthouse
Birth: 1752 Amherst County Virginia
Death: Oct., 1825 Hazel Green Madison County Alabama.
Revolutionary War veteran
Private in the Virginia Militia
Over Mountain Man
Rifleman at battles of King's Mountain and Guilford Courthouse

Robert Higginbotham, along with his wife and children, Henry Boling/Bowling and family, William Garrison and family migrated from Tazewell County, VA c1813 to Madison County, Mississippi Territory.
Robert is buried on land he and his family once owned now known as the Grimwood Farm. aka Cole Bently Farm.

Robert Higginbotham is listed on the DAR Plaque at the entrance of Madison County Courthouse, Huntsville, AL.

The Higginbotham Family is well represented in the State of Alabama and elsewhere in these United States.
