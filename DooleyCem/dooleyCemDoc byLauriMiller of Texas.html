I would love for you to post those surveys.  However, please note that Mr. Hawkins made an error in the name of James Dooley, - he does NOT have a middle initial S - that came from poor spacing on the engraver's part.  His name is just James Dooley (no S).  (Sorry pet peeve of mine).
 
Additional info for you on those listed on Hawkins survey:
 
Thomas P Dooley was son of William and Jane Dooley
M P (Martin Pemberton) was son of William and Jane Dooley, his wife was Mary Joyce, daughter of Peter Joyce and Mary Jennings
Simpson Dooley is not on any family documentation I've ever found - just can't be placed at this time.
 
As for the Cemetery, I would be most grateful for anything that you can do to locate and document the stones there.  I was there for the first time last October as I live in Texas.  It was a dream come true for me after years of research.  We had the dedication of the patriot marker on James Dooley's grave.  The local scout troop cleaned it up and there are some local Dooleys that are making arrangements for it's renovation into a meditation garden setting.  Jim Dooley is the county Tax Assessor and Collector and Eddie Dooley was the former chief of police in Columbia.  The county has agreed to let inmates do the work if the Dooleys feed them and provide supervision and tools.  I so wish I was there to spear head this, but am too far away.  I prod as much as I dare through email.
 
At any rate, I repaired a stone or two when I was there, but did not clean any because they are limestone and fairly brittle.  Any tips on cleaning limestone?  I found a great thing for repairing stones called Rockite and it's a gray epoxy that sets up fairly quick and is like concrete.  There is a second vault that is crushed.  A lady at the Archives remembers playing in that cemetery as a child and might could help clarify things if you want to seek her out.
 
I was told by a local that the graves of slaves also are there, but I've not seen anything to confirm that.  The ground was very uneven and I saw places I believed digging would turn up a stone or remnant.  I tried putting some pieces together, but my time was so limited.  I still grieve not getting to work in that cemetery for several days.  Like you, I knew I could find more if I had tools and time.  Anything you do will be greatly appreciated.  The cemetery was featured last October on the front page of the Columbia Herald.  It is located behind the Grace Nazarene Church on Highland.  Turn down behind the parking lot on a private drive called Grace Lane.  Go to the last house on the left and it is down behind the house.  The church owns that property including houses and has granted easement access to anyone wanting to go to that cemetery.  We were allowed to park down the driveway.  The local Dooleys have gotten it this far, but there is so much more to do.  They have plans to eventually mount the broken stone pieces in a wall with a bench for meditation or some such. 
 
I can give you the name of the most active local (in Nashville) who could maybe help some if you ever need it.
 
Thanks again for all that you do to preserve and protect.
