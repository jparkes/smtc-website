There are five or six graves marked by fieldstones. The massive rock wall around this graveyard is well preserved and has served to keep the cattle out over the years. The cemetery is difficult to reach since there are two gates, normally kept padlocked, on the road from the highway. I was fortunate to find one of the farm workers who let me through the gates. [Fred Lee Hawkins, 1988.] The Rock wall is now fallen in some places and a large tree has fallen into the graveyard and knocked over stones that were not damaged before Mr. Fred Hawkins visited here in 1988 and even John Benderman's visit of 2005. [W.A. 25 May 2010]

*added by Obituary in News Paper. No inscribed marker found.

The initial transcription of the Estes Cemetery was mostly from Maury County Tennessee Cemeteries, by Fred Lee Hawkins, Jr., page 573, Mr Fred visited there 8 Mar 1988. This cemetery is also found in the book They Passed This Way published in about 1967 by Gordon & Lightfoot. Page A-91.
