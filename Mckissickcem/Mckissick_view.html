We are standing on the hill just below the McKisssick Cemetery after crossing the surrounding cemetery fence.  This cemetery is well maintained, however there are many old tombstones that are no longer readable by any expert means.

A view down the row of older stones from the early McKissick family. The headstones on the left and footstones on the right. These stones were very difficult to read but are mostly memorials for the small children of William & F. O. McKissick.

From the western side looking into the cemetery. The ponies were at my back but I was safely across the fence away from them. I am sure they are OK little horses once they are used to you, but like most animals, they do not like strangers.
