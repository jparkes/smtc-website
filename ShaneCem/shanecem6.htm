PHOTOS OF CEMETERIES IN MAURY COUNTY TENNESSEE

Shane Cemetery - Campbell Station

Shown is a major part of the western section of the Cemetery. This is looking northwest from the south side of the cemetery. On the left of this picture area is a large area with many unmarked graves and graves marked with only field stones. There are a few marked graves there. Photo dated 1991 

Broken Stone of Priscilla A Wells (Priscilla A. (Warden)(Kerr) Wells), 1822 - 1887. Photo dated 1992 

Another photo of a major part of the western section of the Cemetery looking northeast. Broken stone of Priscilla Warden in the lower left corner. Her brother's family James & Mary Warden's stones are shown in the far right center of the picture. Photo dated 1992. Families are the Martin's, Kerr's, Warden's, Campbell's Taylor's, Adkisson's, Ussery, Hobbs and many others. Notice the crumbled box tomb on the left which is the memorial of Dora Warden, a child. The Large Box tomb in the upper center to the right of the two small ones is the memorial for Elizabeth Taylor who was Claborn Taylor's spouse.  the next one to the left has no readable inscription. The next still one further one left is the stone of John M. Caskey an infant son of James Caskey. 1992 Photo
