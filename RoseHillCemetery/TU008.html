ROSE HILL CEMETERY, COLUMBIA TENNESSEE

TURNER, Clarence R. (Roosevelt) "Zeb", 5 Oct 1915 - 9 Sep 1962. Tennessee, AOM2 US Navy WWII. (s/o James Anderson & Anna (Austin) Turner. Block "A".
ZEB TURNER DIES SUNDAY IN NASHVILLE.

Monday September 10, 1962. (Columbia) Daily Herald

Clarence R. "Zeb" Turner a veteran of WWII died yesterday (Sunday 9/9/1962) at a Nashville Hospital after suffering a heart attack. Services will be Tuesday at Williams Funeral Home Chapel. Burial will be in Rose Hill Cemetery. The Rev _ _ _ _ Knupp, pastor of Highland Baptist Church is to officiate. The body will lie in state at the funeral home. A native of Maury County Turner was educated in the Columbia Schools. He joined the Navy in 1939 and served as aviation ordnance man aboard the Air Craft Carrier USS Intrepid and served in both the Atlantic & Pacific Theaters. He received a discharge from wounds received when his ship was torpedoed in 1944.

He was the son of Anna Austin Turner and the late James Anderson Turner. In addition to his mother he is survived by three sisters,
Mrs J. W. Sharp - Nashville, Mrs Howell Z. Wantland - Columbia, Mrs. William G. Puckett - Columbia. He is survived by six brothers,
Marshall Turner, Akron OH, William H. Turner, Paul J. Turner, Cecil T. Turner, Marvin M. Turner & Charles A. Turner.
half sister _ _(Mrs?) H.S. Tice.
