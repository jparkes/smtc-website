ROSE HILL CEMETERY, COLUMBIA TENNESSEE

Mr. Ernest Criswell Vick was 75 years of age and a retired truck driver, carpenter and a resident of Blythe Circle. He died Friday Jan. 7, 2000 at Wayne Medical Center in Waynesboro. His Funeral service was conducted Monday Jan. 10, 2000 at 2:30 p.m. at Oaks and Nichols with Wendell Jump officiating. Burial was in the Rose Hill Cemetery. The Maury County native was the son of the late Robert Smith, Sr. and Mary Helen Henson Vick. He was a member of the Church of Christ and was a van driver for the Maury County Senior Citizens for several years. He is survived today by his wife, Mrs. Ola Mae Carroll Vick of Columbia; three daughters, Mrs. Joe (Carolyn) Fults of Lewisburg, Mrs. Beverly Ring of Hohenwald and Mrs. Kathy Hardiman of Columbia; Four sons, Jimmy P. (Barbara) Vick, Sr. and Jerry (Norma) Vick of Spring Hill, Steve Vick and Bobby Vick of Columbia: three sisters, Mrs. Virginia Ashley and Mrs. Alta Dawson of Columbia and Mrs. Imogene Tatum of Pulaski; three brothers, Robert C. (Lucille) Vick, Jr. and Joe (Martha) Vick of Columbia and George (Elizabeth) Vick of Kentucky; thirteen grandchildren and eight grandchildren; several nieces and nephews. Pallbearers were Tim W. Barker, Jimmy P. Vick, Jr., Gerald W. Vick, David Crowell, James Cole, David Carroll, David Barker, and Joe Fults. Honorary pallbearer were Joe Pierce.
