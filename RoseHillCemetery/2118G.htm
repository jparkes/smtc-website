ROSE HILL CEMETERY, COLUMBIA TENNESSEE
 
GARTNER, Tennie T., 19 Jul 1862 - 28 Sep 1898. (d/o J. T. Gartner.) Block "L"

It was with a feeling of deepest sorrow that the numerous friends of Miss Tennie Towler Gartner learned of sad and most untimely death, which occurred last Wednesday morning shortly after 9 o'clock at the house of her sister, Mrs. George Adams, in Nashville. Miss Gartner had been ill for some time and for several weeks past had been in Nashville undergoing medical treatment. At times, her family and friends had hope for her ultimate recovery, but cruel disease had fixed its unrelenting hold upon her, and at last the "angel reaper" came to claim its own and gather her pure sweet spirit into the ever filling yet ever unfilled garner of God... Deceased was the daughter of the late J.T. Gartner and leaves surviving her mother and brothers and sisters, namely -- Messrs. John and James Gartner and Miss Amy Gartner of this city and Mrs. Geo. Adams of Nashville... [T]he funeral was conducted yesterday afternoon at 3:30 o'clock at the family residence, corner North Main and Fifth streets... The burial was at Rose Hill Cemetery, to which resting place a long possession followed the form of the one they loved in life, and as a last loving token of their affection, covered he new made mound with beautiful flowers.... -- Columbia Herald, Sept 30 1898.

Compiled & sent by Mary Bob McClain Richardson. 6 Oct 2015.
