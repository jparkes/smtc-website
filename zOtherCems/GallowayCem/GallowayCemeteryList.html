GALLOWAY CEMETERY, (Gravel Hill Road)(NW.MAURY COUNTY TENNESSEE

Locator Map

 

GALLOWAY, Enoch, 5 Dec 1784 - Sep 1867. (born in North Carolina; migrated to Maury Co in 1809 and married Anna Beale Taylor (1782-1835). They had two sons Matthew and Uriah and may have had other children. After Anna's death, Enoch married Elizabeth Trimble in 1836. During the "War of 1812" Enoch served as a Private in the 2nd Regiment of the West Tennessee Militia. Ref: Sam Sproles email to Mary Bob.)
GALLOWAY, Anna, 1782 - 1835. (Nee: Beale; b. Giles Co., Tn.; w/o Enoch Galloway.)
GALLOWAY, Matthew J., 5 May 1813 - 16 Apr 1889. (Name on stone: Gallaway; s/o Enoch & Anna Beale Galloway; h/o Susan Williamson Galloway.)
GALLOWAY, Susan V., 14 Nov 1817 - 28 May 1900. (Stone Broken; w/o Matthew J. Galloway; d/o George C. Williamson of Culleoka of this county.)
GALLOWAY, S. K., 11 Mar 1841 - 11 May 1909. (Stone Broken.)

There are two or three tombstones marked with fieldstones.  

This cemetery listing was  published in the various books of the past. The last one Mr. Fred Lee Hawkins in the book "Maury County Tennessee Cemeteries" listed this cemetery on page 130. He visited  there 10 Feb 1987. Prior to that it was listed in the book "They Passed This Way" page B-68, about 1963.  Transcription provided by Mary Bob McClain, complied from various sources.
