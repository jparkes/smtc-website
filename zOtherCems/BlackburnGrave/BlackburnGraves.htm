BLACKBURN GRAVES, Lasea Rd, MAURY COUNTY TENNESSEE

From Columbia go out Bear Creek Pike 6 and 6 tenths of a mile and turn north on Lasea Rd and go 287 yards or .16 tenths of a mile and look to your left on the bank of the road under a Cedar. There will see the single footstone standing. The stone is about 15 to 20 yards due west from the center of the Lasea Rd. The headstone is on the ground lying on the grave, but the footstone is the same size and standing. The cemetery is currently being maintained as a part of a extended lawn. Mr. Fred Hawkins said this was the Collins Home in 1986.

The lone standing footstone for William Blackburn, 1772-1856.

BLACKBURN, William, 4 Oct 1772 - 14 May 1856. 

There evidently was fieldstones here marking graves in past times. Mention was made of that previously but no sign of them today. It is possible they were removed to make way for mowing the area or they might just have sunken into the ground. These along with other less apt possibilities exist as to why there are no fieldstones here in 2009.
Listed in "Maury County Tennessee Cemeteries", 10 Apr 1986, by Fred Lee Hawkins, page 92. Listed also in "They Passed This Way"  Page C-41 about 1963. Other information & mapping graphics by Wayne Austin 23 Dec 2008. Added here 10 Feb 2009.
