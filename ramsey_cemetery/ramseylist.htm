This cemetery though small is well laid out in even rows. The number to the left of the listing represents the sequence of the photograph which denotes the proximity of the graves except in cases where the photographer changed to the next row. The photographer started on the Southwest Corner of the cemetery and went north up the first row and south on the second row and north on the third row etc..  #31 Eliza J Coffee stone was the first individual stone photo made in the cemetery going north.  #38 James W. Coffey stone was the last photograph on the first row. However there are other unmarked graves in row #1. The skip in row 1 between #38 and #40 indicates three possible unmarked graves or stones that have been lost and/or sunken below the ground.  I did not probe for the lost memorials. Other skips are meaningless in regards to cemetery analysis. #40 John G. Ramsey is the first stone going south on the second row. #53 Margaret Isabella Ramsey memorial photo is the first one on the third row going back north. #59 Joseph B. Ramsey is the last marked stone in Row #3. Row # 3 & Row #4 consist of many fieldstones or unmarked graves or possible unused plots.
