Joseph Ballanfant was born 14 Nov 1790 in Halifax County, Virginia, according to Ballanfant Family Bible. Note that his tombstone at Ballanfant Cemetery shows his birth date as 14 Nov 1795. His parents were Jean �John� Bellenfant and Louisa �Lucy� Yeoman. At a young age, his family moved to Rockingham County, North Carolina, where he grew up. He served in the War of 1812 for six months, beginning 28 Nov 1814. Sometime after the War he moved to Williamson County, Tennessee. During his time in Williamson County, he married Sarah �Sally� Turner. The family Bible records the following: "Joseph Ballanfant eldest son of John Ballanfant, a native of France and Sally Turner fifth daughter of James Turner were married by Rev'd Garner McConnico in Williamson County, Tennessee on the 22nd day of April A D 1819". He emigrated in 1824 to the Culleoka area of Maury County, Tennessee where he remained until his death. Joseph was a trustee of the first school at Culleoka in 1835. Pleasant Grove (Culleoka) Post Office was opened at the Ballanfant estate in about 1835 and remained there for ten years. Family history and sources show that Joseph and Sally only had one son, John, but the 1830 census shows a young girl under 5 years of age in the household. If she was a daughter, she might have died young.
