He also lists several unmarked graves. There is an unmarked grave he doesn't know about. Between the graves of Claiborne L. I am copying my sister, Susan Dedman Weis on this in case she has any other information.

I thought John Akin's wife, Mary Watson Howe Akin, was buried near John at Pisgah, but she is not on the list. I may be mistaken about this as she and John were both living in Wayne County at the time of their deaths. She predeceased John. John was brought to Pisgah, and I thought Mary was also. I thought she was buried to the left of John's grave--to the left of the slot where John's original stone stood before collapsing. Again, I am going on long ago information which may be incorrect. Wayne's work is quite thorough.

Where you have coverage I find yours and Wayne's work to be more useful than FindAGrave. Thanks for all the hard work. Ancestry.com is riddled with mistakes too. Too many folks going for volume at the expense of accuracy.

The James W. Ridley I was asking about was the son of James Webb Ridley and grandson of James Webb Smith Ridley, and who was the son of James Webb Ridley and Madge Whitney. James Whitney Ridley committed suicide in Columbia in 1929. I have seen references to his being buried at Rosehill, but I can't find any record of that. After her divorce from James Webb Ridley, Madge Whitney Ridley married James Edwin Dedman, brother of Joseph Marcus Dedman. James Edwin and Madge Whitney are buried in Birmingham, AL.
