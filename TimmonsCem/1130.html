TIMMONS CEMETERY, Santa Fe Pike, MAURY COUNTY, TENNESSEE

James K.P. & Mary E. Timmons memorial. 

James K.P. Timmons inscription. "Peaceful be thy silent slumber, the battle fought the victory won, thy warfare's o'er. Christain labor done."  

TIMMONS, James K. P., 5 Apr 1841 - 6 Sep 1914. (Son S. H. & Malinda Sellers Timmons.) C.S.A.
TIMMONS, Mary E., 2 Apr 1848 - 5 Mar 1930. (Wife J. K. P. Timmons; daughter of Jesse & Martha J. Amis Evans.)
JAMES Knox Polk Timmons was born at Ashwood on 5 Apr 1841, the son of S. H. & Melinda Sellers Timmons. During the Civil War he fought in Co. A, 9th Tenn. Cav. Bn. & in Co.E, 8th Tenn Infantry. After the war, he returned to Maury Co., married Miss Mary B. Evans on 13 Jul 1865 and opened a mercantile establishment near Godwin. He was postmaster of the local office after 1878 and the agency received the name 'Timmons' after J.K.P. He was the father of five children.
