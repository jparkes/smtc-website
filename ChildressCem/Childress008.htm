CHILDRESS CEMETERY, BRYANT STATION, MAURY COUNTY TENNESSEE

The large tree in this photo perhaps hovers over the lost grave of Hardin Mayberry.

MAYBERRY, Hardin, born 1856 - death unknown. Husband of Cornelia E. Gallaway, Thought to be buried here.
Hardin Mayberry was the son of Gabriel and Rosanna (Hardin) Mayberry. He was born in Hickman County, Tennessee in 1826. He first married Cornelia E., the daughter of James E. & Marion Galloway. Cornelia died in 1858 and Hardin then married America Hardin Blair, widow of G. H. Blair and daughter of Pleasant G. & Tabitha Hardin. Mr. Mayberry served for a time as a soldier in the Confederate States Army. Several of his children and grandchildren are also buried in this small cemetery.
A big oak tree has grown up beside the grave of Cornelia, wife of Hardin Mayberry, and may be over the resting place of Hardin Mayberry. We can find no stone for Hardin, but concede it may be possible that it is inside the trunk of the large tree and will be revealed someday when the tree dies and rots away. 
[condensed from the commentary of Fred Lee Hawkins in the book Maury County Tennessee Cemeteries, Page 55. 1988, by WA 7 Jun 2008]
