Larry Wayne Turner, Jul 24, 1944 - May 19, 2015, d. Columbia, Tennessee  Jul 24, 1944 - May 19, 2015, d. Columbia, Tennessee, s/o Paul Johnson Turner and Pauline Goosby Turner. 1962 graduate of Columbia Central High Sch..

Larry Wayne Turner, 70, retired line foreman with Columbia Power & Water System and resident of Tom Osborne Road, died Tuesday, May 19, 2015 at Vanderbilt Medical Center.

Funeral services will be conducted Friday at 11:00 A.M. at Highland Church of Christ with David Morris and Barry Throneberry officiating. Burial will follow in Polk Memorial Gardens. The family will visit with friends Thursday from 3:00 � 8:00 P.M. at Oakes & Nichols Funeral Home and Friday from 10:00 � 11:00 A.M. at the church.

The family suggests memorials to Highland Church of Christ Building Fund, 497 East James Campbell Blvd., Columbia, TN 38401. Condolences may be extended online at www.oakesandnichols.com.

The Maury native was the son of the late Paul Johnson Turner and Pauline Goosby Turner. He was a 1962 graduate of Columbia Central High School and continued to be very active with the Columbia Band Boosters. He had coached American Little League Baseball for eight years and was an antique car enthusiast. Mr. Turner was a member of Highland Church of Christ, where he was a former deacon and had participated on several mission trips to City of Children in Ensenada, Mexico.

The loving husband, father, and grandfather, affectionately known as "Pop", is survived by his wife of fifty years, Anita Carol Brewer Turner of Columbia; sons, Scott (Gina) Turner of Columbia; Brent (Katie) Turner of Lascassas; grandchildren, Trevor (Amber) Turner, Morgan Turner, Owen Turner, Malli Shea Turner, Jonathan Turner, Anna Turner, Jeremiah Turner; great granddaughter, Lily Elizabeth Turner; sister, Phyllis (Jack) Powers of McKinney, Texas; brother, Ronnie (Sandy) Turner of Columbia; and several nieces and nephews.

Active pallbearers will be Scott Turner, Brent Turner, Trevor Turner, Morgan Turner, Owen Turner, and Buddy Stoops. Honorary pallbearers include Elders and Deacons of Highland Church of Christ, past and present employees of Columbia Power & Water Systems, McDonald's Breakfast Group, Toy Box Group, Paul & Kathy Prince, Joe & Amy Colvett, Bob & Ann White, Dennis & Brenda Edwards, Craig & Phyllis Holley, Paul & Polly Keesling, Don & Jean Westmoreland, Jim & Bennie Pilkinton, John & Jackie Markham, Bobby & Glenna Thurman, Briggs Primm, Walter Main, David Holmes, Elaine Stoops and Paula Woody..
