LYNNWOOD CEMETERY, Lynnville, GILES COUNTY, TENNESSEE
HARDCASTLE, Charles Gregory "Greg", Age 34 died 24 Sep 2008 in the Yokley Community of Giles County. He was a resident of Nashville, native of Williamson County Franklin Tennessee. s/o Rosemary (Hubbell) & stepson of Dr. Thad Porter. (This listing precedes any monument that may be set in place.)

Charles Gregory "Greg" Hardcastle
Charles Gregory "Greg" Hardcastle, 34, of Nashville and graduate of Franklin Road Academy and the University of Tennessee in Knoxville, died September 24, 2008. He worked as a computer specialist in the Nashville area.
Survivors include his parents, Rosemary and (stepson of) (Dr) Lester Porter (Cancer Surgeon) of Nashville; sisters, Beth (Greg) Lawrence and Lacey Porter of Nashville;
maternal grandmother, Mary Austin Hubbell of Columbia; uncle, Charles L. (Joyce) Hubbell of Lynnville; cousins, Jeff (Susan) Hubbell of Murfreesboro, Jamie (Penny) Hubbell, and Cyndy (Paul) Gibson of Columbia. Family services were private. Greg was the son of Loy Hardcastle.

Greg purchased a gun and drove south from his residence in Nashville to the old Dairy Farm homeplace of his grandparents the Gardner and Mary Austin Hubbell's to end his life. It is sad that he chose a place of his fond memories for that.
Charles Hubbell who farms the old family farm found his body. He had spoken to Greg as he pulled up into the old farm and thought everything was OK. So he went on to the back side of his place near Gibsonville Cemetery to check on his cattle. He heard the gun shot and went when he returned he found the body in back of the house.

He was cremated and the ashes maintained for a time then eventually buried (19 Sep 2010) beside his grandparents Gardner & Mary Hubbell in Lynnville Cemetery just south of Gardner's grave. Mary (Austin) Hubbell died 16 Sep 2010.Â
