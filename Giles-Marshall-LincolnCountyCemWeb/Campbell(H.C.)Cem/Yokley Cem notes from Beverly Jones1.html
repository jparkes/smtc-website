Morristown Cemetery
 
     My vague memory doesn't include Morristown cemetery being on a hill.  I think we parked on the road immediately adjacent to the cemetery.
I will ask my cousin Porter Hannah where it is and how to get there.
     I THINK the Campbell-Rittenberry cemetery is on the land of Mark Dunavant, who is the person I have recently written to about the
Margaret Hannah mystery.  Mark and I are related through the Hannahs and the Campbells. He's a walking encyclopedia of local history, and very entertaining.
The name for Campbell-Rittenberry cemetery in the cemetery book is H.C. Campbell Cemetery, but I started using Campbell-Rittenberry because of the sign
on the gate.  You could be a trendsetter if you add West!  I read about the Rittenberrys recently.  They all lived in Alabama and seem like remarkable
people.  I ended up wondering why they were buried in Giles County, Tennessee.
     I sure wish I had had a bigger vision of what I was doing in 2002.  I could have photographed all five graves at Morristown.  I was a beginner.
I'm going to give another shot at figuring out who the mystery grave might be, but your process of elimination will be the key.  Did you find a lot
of field stones in Yokley Cemetery like Terina did? 
     Mark Dunavant may be the go-to person about the sorry condition of Yokley Cemetery.  I think I mentioned it in my letter.  Being a local landowner,
he may have standing to get something done about it (without having to sue----cheaper).
Later...
Beverly
