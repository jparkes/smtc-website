Several unmarked graves. Unmarked fieldstones. 3615/3617
 

I don't know what the situation is regarding the missing stones of this cemetery. Neither I nor anyone lately have been able to find any monuments to the other folks grouped into this cemetery. Maybe the other stones are located away from the above three monuments and no one saw them. I did look around, but sometimes in these old graveyards stones can be scattered a 100 yards apart with nothing in between. The area of this cemetery is really just a part of a tree lined thicket which extends north and south. One would think all the Isom kin would be found in one area of this cemetery but I cannot conclude that. Either the old historical cemetery listers were capturing the others as unmarked graves or something is amiss here regarding grave locations.
