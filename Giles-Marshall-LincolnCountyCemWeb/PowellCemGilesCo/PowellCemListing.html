*I can's say for sure these were wild hogs but as far as I could see there were no fences to contain them. The big male had a lot of features of the wild hog here in the south. Long brown hair tusk and strong digging type snout. They were noisy critters and I was glad for the chain link fence between me and them. I think they were wild, but the farmer nearby may be feeding them and harvesting meat from the pack. He has his job cut out to keep them contained because they multiply like rats. There was a nearby female that had many small piglets. As I took his photo the big male and I had a staring contest and if he wanted he could have sent me scampering for cover to the truck if he had attacked the wire fence.

**Land for this cemetery was donated by Calvin H. Powell d. 1858.
