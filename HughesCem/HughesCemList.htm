HUGHES CEMETERY, (Santa Fe Pike, Highway 7), MAURY COUNTY TENNESSEE
aka ZELLNER CEMETERY

Mapping the location

BYNUM, Elizabeth, 31 May 1803 - 17 Oct 1878. (75-04-17.) "Wife of Buffin Bynum.'
DUKE, Kessiah, 1797 - 2 Apr 1863. (Age 66 yrs.) 'Wife of Green Duke.' (See Williamson Co., Tn. Mar-
riages: Green Duke to Kerziah Timmons, 1 Jan 1817, by Donaldson Potter, Magestrate.)
GOAD, Fannie Ann, 8 Jun 1814 - 10 Jun 1860. (46-00-02.) 'Wife Wiley Goad.' (nee Frances A. Timmons;
1st married Sample Carrigan, 5 Feb 1843; 2nd married Wiley T. Goad on 14 Jun 1857.)
*GOAD, Mrs. Emily, "aged, d. Sunday; Baptist; buried at Hughes cemetery." (Columbia Herald, 17 Mar 1899.)
*GOAD, Tom, married, b. 21 Nov 1851, d. 17 Sep 1926; buried in Hughes Cem.; son of Joe & Miss Bynum Goad.
HAMLETT, Berrymon, d. 23 Oct 1867. (Aged 66 yrs.)
HUGHES, Elizabeth, 30 Jan 1795 - 10 Aug 1852.
HUGHES, J. M., 29 Aug 1821 - 12 Dec 1882. (61-03-14.)
HUGHES, Sarah Rebecca, 4 Jan 1826 - 21 Jan 1904. (Wife of J. M. Hughes.)
HUGHES, Lucy E., 15 Apr 1848 - 8 Jan 1849. (Daughter of J. M. & S. R. Hughes.)
HUGHES, Martha, 29 Jan 1861 - 13 Sep 1898. (35-02-08. Daughter of J. M. & S. R. Hughes.)
HUGHES, Lettie Jane, 29 Dec 1863 - 3 Jan 1884. (20-00-05; Daughter of J. M. & S. R. Hughes)
HUGHES, Joseph Barnette, 12 Jun 1866 - 20 Aug 1896. (30-02-08; Son of J. M. & S. Rebecca Hughes; died of Typhoid; obituary in Columbia Herald, 21 Aug 1896.)
*HUGHES, Miss Missie, 'd. 13 Sep 1896 near Godwin; daughter of Mrs. Rebecca Hughes; ill 3 reeks with typhoid, 2nd child taken from mother in month, interred in Family Burial Grounds' (Columbia Herald, 18 Sep 1896.)
LIPSCOMB, (Zellner), Infant son of David & Margaret Zellner Lipscomb, no dates. born Sep 1863 died cir Jun 1864. Parents rode two days in a buggy back to Maury County from Nashville to bury this child in a hand made cedar casket lined with flannel.David Lipscomb's name is penned to David Lipscomb College in Nashville, a highly acclaimed Christian College.  
*McCURRY, Mrs. Mary Lizzie, "d. Thurs, near Godwin; married to C. M. McCurry; Hus. & 5 children survive; d. 17 Nov. of heart failure." (Daily Herald, 18 Nov 1904.)
NOTGRASS, Sallie R., d. 22 Jan 1934. (Aged 78 yrs.; Wife of Pen Notgrass; Daughter of Wm. & Lizzie Allen Estes; undertaker's marker.)
*NOTGRASS, Mrs. Emma, "d. Wed., 75 years; mother of Penn Notgrass and sister of late J. M. Hughes; buried at Hughes Graveyard." (Columbia Herald, 10 Jun 1904.)
PARTEE, Abner, 31 Oct 1772 - 24 Aug 1843.
PARTEE, Mary, d. 15 Feb 1837. (56-02-15) (Consort of Abner Partee.)
SHURMAN, Mary L., 19 Sep 1832 (Age 10 mos.) (Inf. daughter of C. W. & Mary Shurman. Only footstone with "M.L.S."
was found, taken from T.P.T.W.)

marriages: William Timmons to Hane Culen, 18 Dec 1794.)
TIMMONS, William, Footstone "W.T." beside Hannah Timmons.
TIMMONS, Thomas D., 7 Feb 1810 - 12 Mar 1869. (Only footstone with 'T.D.T.' found in 1987, inscription from 1963 in the book, "They Passed This Way.")
ZELLNER, Henry, 28 Feb 1814 - 4 Nov 1899.

 *persons thought to be buried here due to evidence from historical articles, though no stone existed in 1987.

"This cemetery has been abandoned for a long time. Some of the graves are enclosed inside an iron fence. That part is badly grown up. Outside this fence, cattle have tumbled many stones, broken others and trampled underground still more of them." F.L.H., 1987.

Published in "Maury County Tennessee Cemeteries", Page 175, by Fred Lee Hawkins, 3 Feb 1987 and in "They Passed This Way" by Marise Lightfoot, and Evelyn Shackelford, Page B-49, 1964.
