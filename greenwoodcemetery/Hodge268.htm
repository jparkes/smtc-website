Diane as a rule we on this site use the dates according to the tombstone because those can survive long after any paper records are gone. We do that unless we get overwhelming evidence to the contrary. There are two problems in applying this procedure in this case, (1) we don't have a (double checked) proven record of the tombstone (dates unreadable above and on findagrave.com) inscription nor (2) do we have that for the family Bible even though your presentation evidence is strong. In light of that contradiction I am staying with the original information which can be verified against a book as noted on this site. Also I can see a faint 1789 in the graphic above. So the reader can make up their own mind, but I suppose if your doing genealogy you will use 1793.  C. Wayne Austin 2 Jan 2014
