HAYNES CEMETERY (a.k.a. FLOWERS CHAPEL or SYMRNA CEMETERY.), MAURY COUNTY TENNESSEE

ALLEN, John H., born 1811, died 1878.
and
ALLEN, Anne Freeland, 25 Feb 1816 - 1885, (Wife John H., only fieldstone markers. Dates given by her daughter, Margaret Allen Parrish. Dates in Century Review of Maury Co. as detailed on page 138 but translated by WA.)

M. A. Parrish was born, May, 1840 and took up arms for the Confederate cause, was taken prisoner at Ft. Donelson, and died before being exchanged. His wife Mrs. Margaret Allen Parrish was born near Pulaski, daughter of John H. Allen, who was born in N. C., 1811, and died in 1878. Her mother, Anna Freeland Allen, born, 1817, died in 1885; in 1836 she married Mr. John H. Allen, who had come from North Carolina to Maury County four years prior or during 1832. Mrs. Parrish resides with J. D. Frierson at Ridley."
